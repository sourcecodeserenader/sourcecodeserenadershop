About Me

My journey from tinkerer to tech creative.

1995

Born in Battle Creek, MI. Surrounded by the newest gadgets from day one, I spent my childhood tearing apart electronics and dreaming up new creations, building art installations and homemade instruments before I ever knew what code was. By grade school I was crafting Flash games, hand-coding HTML/CSS sites, and designing elaborate MySpace layouts complete with custom chat rooms, galleries, and blogs. Along the way I taught myself Photoshop, animated manga scans, and all the skills I now bring to web development

2015

When Isabella arrived, I discovered my greatest calling: creating a safe, nurturing world for my daughter. Between late night coding sessions and bedtime stories, I learned to balance structure with creativity and let patience, empathy, and resilience become my new daily workflow

2019–2021

Between Rebecca’s arrival in 2019 and Ambrielle’s in 2021, I was nursing two babies around the clock while wearing my preschool and theater teacher hat for the local school district, and still tackling my own college coursework. I designed end-to-end lesson plans (complete with custom videos, interactive presentations, and hands on activity sheets) that delighted both my students and my daughters. Juggling diapers, drama rehearsals, and deadlines taught me to streamline creativity under pressure, and reminded me to celebrate every tiny victory

2022

I threw myself into SNHU’s computer science program like a kid in a candy store... except the candy was cybersecurity puzzles, scalable software challenges, and end to end system designs. I spent countless late nights fueled by strong coffee and stronger curiosity, leading impromptu hackathons in my dorm room and rallying classmates for weekend code sprints. Every group project felt like building a tiny tech startup: wireframing features at sunrise, wrestling with APIs by moonlight, and celebrating that sweet moment when everything finally clicks. Those marathon sessions didn’t just sharpen my skills; they taught me how to collaborate, adapt on the fly, and turn a jumble of requirements into polished, secure applications I can be proud of

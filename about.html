About Me

My journey from tinkerer to tech creative.

1995

Surrounded by the latest gadgets from day one, I spent my early years tearing apart electronics, crafting art installations and homemade instruments—long before I even knew what “code” was. By grade school I was hand-coding Flash games, building elaborate MySpace layouts with custom chat rooms, galleries, and blogs, and teaching myself Photoshop & manga animation—all skills I now bring to web development.

2015

When Isabella arrived, I discovered my greatest calling: creating a safe, nurturing world for my daughter. Between late-night coding sprints and bedtime stories, I learned to balance structure with creativity—and to let patience, empathy, and resilience become my new daily workflow.

2019–2021

Between Rebecca’s arrival in ’19 and Ambrielle’s in ’21, I was nursing two babies 24/7 while teaching preschool & theater, and tackling my own college coursework. I designed end-to-end lesson plans—complete with videos, interactive slides, and hands-on activities—that delighted both my students and daughters. Juggling diapers, drama rehearsals, and deadlines taught me to streamline creativity under pressure and celebrate every tiny victory.

2022

I dove into SNHU’s CS program like a kid in a candy store—except the candy was cybersecurity puzzles, scalable software challenges, and system designs. Countless late-night hackathons fueled by coffee sharpened my skills and taught me the power of teamwork: wireframing features at dawn, wrangling APIs by moonlight, and turning chaos into polished, secure applications I’m proud to ship.
